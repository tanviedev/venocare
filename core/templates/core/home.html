What is CVI?

CVI occurs when the valves in the veins of your legs become damaged or weakened, causing blood to pool instead of flowing back to your heart properly. This leads to symptoms like swelling, pain, and skin changes.

Symptoms

  • Swelling in the lower legs and ankles
  • Aching or heaviness in the legs
  • Varicose veins
  • Skin discoloration or ulcers
  • Itching or burning sensations

Understanding Chronic Venous Insufficiency

Chronic Venous Insufficiency is a common condition affecting millions worldwide, where the veins in your legs struggle to send blood back to your heart efficiently. Early detection and management can greatly improve quality of life.

Causes & Risk Factors

CVI can be caused by valve damage, blood clots, obesity, pregnancy, prolonged standing, or family history.

Diagnosis

Doctors diagnose CVI with physical exams and imaging tests such as Doppler ultrasound.

Treatment Options

Treatment ranges from lifestyle changes, compression stockings, medications, to surgical interventions depending on severity.

Prevention Tips

  • Exercise regularly to improve leg circulation
  • Avoid prolonged standing or sitting
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Elevate legs when resting
